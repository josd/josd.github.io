Bell/CHSH — local realism vs quantum predictions
============================================

Local-realist hidden variables (LRHV) imply the CHSH bound:
  For any deterministic λ:  s(λ) = A0B0 + A0B1 + A1B0 − A1B1
                          = A0(B0+B1) + A1(B0−B1) ∈ {−2, 2}.
  Averaging over λ cannot exceed 2 ⇒ |S| ≤ 2 in all LRHV models.  □

Quantum prediction for the singlet (E = −cos angle-difference):
  Angles: A0=0.0°, A1=90.0°, B0=45.0°, B1=-45.0°
  This gives |S| = 2√2 ≈ 2.828, violating the LRHV bound.  □

Classical (LRHV) check by enumeration:
  Distinct s(λ) values over 16 assignments: [-2, 2]
  Max |s(λ)| = 2  ⇒  any convex average obeys |S| ≤ 2.

Quantum (singlet) check:
  E(A0,B0)=-0.707106781187, E(A0,B1)=-0.707106781187, E(A1,B0)=-0.707106781187, E(A1,B1)=0.707106781187
  S_QM = -2.828427124746,   2√2 ≈ 2.828427124746

Harness:
  • LRHV extreme points yield s ∈ {−2, 2} ⇒ |S| ≤ 2 for any LRHV theory. ✓
  • Quantum singlet with standard angles gives |S| = 2√2 > 2. ✓
Conclusion:
  The CHSH/Bell experiment rules out local-realist hidden-variable assignments.
  Nature violates |S| ≤ 2; experiments find |S| ≈ 2.8, in line with quantum mechanics. □
