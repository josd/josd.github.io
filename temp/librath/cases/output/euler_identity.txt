Answer
------
e^{iπ} + 1 = 0.

Reason why
----------
A) Power series:
   exp(z)=Σ z^n/n!,  cos x=Σ (-1)^k x^{2k}/(2k)!,  sin x=Σ (-1)^k x^{2k+1}/(2k+1)!
   Put z=ix. Even/odd terms separate, using i^2=-1, giving exp(ix)=cos x + i sin x.
   With x=π: exp(iπ)=cos π + i sin π = -1 + 0·i ⇒ exp(iπ)+1=0.

B) ODE uniqueness:
   f(x)=exp(ix) solves f'=i f, f(0)=1.  g(x)=cos x + i sin x also solves g'=i g, g(0)=1.
   The IVP y'=i y, y(0)=1 has a unique solution, hence f≡g and exp(ix)=cos x + i sin x.
   Therefore exp(iπ)=-1. □
