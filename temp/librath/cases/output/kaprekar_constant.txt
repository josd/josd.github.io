1/ Answer
- Kaprekar’s constant (6174) is reached by 9990/10000 numbers.
- Maximum iterations required: 7
- Non-converging cases (rep-digits): 10

2/ Reason why
- Kaprekar’s routine: sort digits descending and ascending (keeping leading zeros), then subtract.
- All 4-digit numbers except the rep-digits eventually land on 6174 and then stay there.
- Rep-digits (e.g., 1111) produce 0000 in one step and get stuck at 0000; they never reach 6174.
